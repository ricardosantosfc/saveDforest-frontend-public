The saveDforest Game Project

The saveDforest game is a cross-disciplinary inter-university project involving a Masters' thesis in Informatics Engineering.

Its main goal is to change players' everyday behavior so they contribute to saving forests and mitigate the effects of already damaged ones.

To achieve this goal, the game promotes empathy towards animals and people who are directly affected by deforestation.

The current version of the game focuses on the Borneo forest's inhabitants, and also raises awareness for the global effects of the forest's destruction.

How the Game Works

The game consists of multiple scenarios with dialogues, questions and challenges. The player chooses what to answer, what to learn, and what to do.

Scenarios are built with a threefold purpose:

1. Provide information on climate change and the perils of world forests;

2. Trigger empathy for the forest animals and people who depend on the forest;

3. Spur the interest in knowing more about the forest, the climate crisis, and pro-environmental behavior.
